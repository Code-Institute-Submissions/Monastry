OUR STORY

Our Beginnings

Open as of March 2015, the Monastry has been delivering quality service with historically-detailed rooms, aromatic Japanese dishes and local cocktails. Having a renewed appearance with the renovation in 2018, the Monastry is always delighted to serve you and provide you with a timeless experience.

The Monastry was built in 1943 on top of Fort Canning Hill as part of a strategic outpost for the British in the fight of World War 2. Apart from being a useful bunker for strategic planning and operations, the monastry has been involved as the central heart of operations for trade during those difficult times. Today, although the military appearance has disappeared, the Monastry has been restored as a modern compound,focusing on a rich culture and heritage.
